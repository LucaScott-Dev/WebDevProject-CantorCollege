Rules Governing the Use of the Library and Resource Centre

Introduction

All students and staff of the College are entitled to register as members of the Library and Resource Centre and to use the facilities for the purpose of their course of study or College employment.

Membership may also be extended to other individuals or to persons nominated from outside organisations; a fee is normally charged. Access for study purposes may also be granted to non-members by arrangement. Details are available in the External Services leaflet.

Use of the facilities implies acceptance of the Library and Resource Centre rules. These rules protect the interests of all users and ensure that the College can operate within the law and function effectively. They apply at all sites, including workstation rooms and site libraries.

Admission to the Library and Resource Centre is conditional upon observing these rules, which staff are empowered to enforce. Breaches may be dealt with through Library disciplinary action or, if appropriate, through the College’s student or staff procedures.

Rules

A. Access

  1. The use of Library and Resource Centre facilities is normally limited to educational and related purposes of the College.
  2. All individuals wishing to become members must comply with registration procedures, including an undertaking to abide by the rules.
  3. Individuals or organisations not eligible for membership may be admitted for study purposes only, by arrangement. They must sign the Visitors’ Book and may be required to show identification. Study access does not include use of the College Academic Network.
  4. ID cards are non-transferable. The named person is responsible for all items borrowed and liable for replacement costs in cases of loss or damage.

    Users must be willing to show ID cards or other identification when requested by staff.

B. Use of Facilities

  1. The Library is not responsible for loss of, or damage to, personal belongings. Users must not leave belongings unattended. Study tables or PCs may not be reserved using personal items. Unattended items may be removed by staff and can be collected at Reception.
  2. All users must conduct themselves quietly. Conversation should be kept to a minimum. Silent areas must be respected.
  3. Personal stereos may not be used in the Library and Resource Centre.
  4. Mobile phones must be turned off in all study areas. Their use is permitted only in the enclosed stairwells of the Main Site building.
  5. Users must not use mains electrical supplies for personal equipment unless it has been tested and certified as meeting Health & Safety standards. Written evidence of compliance is required.
  6. Eating and drinking are not permitted in public areas of the Library and Resource Centre.
  7. Action will be taken against anyone misusing furniture, facilities, or materials. Offenders may be required to pay for repair or replacement.
